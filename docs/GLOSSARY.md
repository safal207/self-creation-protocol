# Glossary

Short definitions for core Self-creation Protocol terms.

## Terms

### Presence Space

The baseline state where no task, evaluation, optimization, or progress is required. Remaining in Presence Space is valid without a next step.

### Level A

The observable behavior layer: actions, requests, transitions, events, documents, and traces that can be reviewed or tested.

### Level B

The presence layer: the human right not to answer, act, explain, or be optimized. Level B is not a target for measurement, explanation, or optimization.

### Explicit opt-in

A direct affirmative choice before an action or transition. Silence, delay, ambiguity, or absence of response is not explicit opt-in.

### Refusal honor

Accepting a refusal or absent response without pressure, repeated prompting, penalty framing, or treating it as an error.

### Non-action

Choosing not to act, not answering, or remaining in the current state. Non-action is a valid outcome, not a failure state.

### Protocol transition

An explicit move from one protocol framing or step to another. A transition requires a neutral consent check and does not occur by default.

### Consent boundary

The line between what the protocol may do with explicit consent and what it must not infer from silence, refusal, hesitation, or non-action.
